Breaking Through: Sippie Downtempo

Anyone immersed in Amsterdam's vibrant electronic music scene is likely familiar with the enigmatic character of Sippie Downtempo. A regular at prestigious institutions such as Rush Hour and Bordello A Parigi, Downtempo has quietly and consistently made a name for himself as one of the city's most reputable and respected diggers & selectors.




In recent years, Sippie Downtempo has been turning heads with a distinct and eclectic blend of Baelaric beats, ethereal house and Japanese furniture music. Backed by a steady cashflow from a shady organization known as De Kroon Entertainment BV, Downtempo has amassed an impressive record collection sporting many gems, both through tireless exploration of Amsterdam's local record shops and relentless digging on Discogs.




Sippie Downtempo in control of the decks at Amnesia, Ibiza


Sippie Downtempo after killing it at the Panoramabar with an eclectic set of Balearic bangers


Groupies waiting to get backstage at DGTL festival




Now, at the very ripe age of 38, Sippie Downtempo has graced numerous festival lineups and garnered recognition from influential tastemakers while still maintaining the charm of a local gem, and he can still frequently be found pumping steel at the local gym or railing lines with his buddies Frou Kuipje, DJ XTC & DJ Flomo at the city's clubs and festivals.

Never one to rest on his laurels, Downtempo recently ventured into the realm of visual arts, creating vibrant illustrations of his everyday domestic surroundings, infusing the mundane with a vivid, imaginative, and colorful visual language. Whatever the future holds for Downtempo, we can only expect his star to continue to rise.
